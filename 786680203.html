Frame Cooling

The frame cooling is a stream of ambient temperature gas which is applied to the probe's frame. This is intended as a cooling of the electric parts in high RF and high duty cycle experiments. It is as well used to keep the probe's electric parts at ambient temperature in high or low temperature experiments. Frame cooling is switched on by ticking the corresponding check box. The target frame cooling pressure may be set by clicking the Set button and typing in the desired value.

The frame cooling may be used for other purposes, e. g. for transfer line cooling.

NOTE

Please note that at high spinning speed (typically close to the maximum rotor dependent spinning speed), the rotor may heat up. In such cases cooling is required to keep the sample at a suitable temperature.
